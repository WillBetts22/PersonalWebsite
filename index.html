Axiomatic Faith: What Rudin Taught Me About God

Author: William Betts

An essay on the surprising parallel between mathematical axioms and theological faith. After a year of proofs and a year of scripture, I argue that certainty in any discipline begins with a leap — and that the difference between mathematics and theology is not whether that leap was taken, but whether we are willing to admit it.

An Investigation of the Quadratic Forms

Authors: Jia Hao Liang, Skylar Tian, Will Betts
Date: February 1, 2026

Abstract: This paper introduces the geometry and linear-algebraic structure of quadratic forms, focusing on their symmetric matrix representation, orthogonal diagonalization, definiteness, and role in optimization. We reinterpret classical identities of Diophantus, Brahmagupta, Euler, and Degen as compositions of quadratic forms, leading to the Hurwitz-Radon theorem. The paper also extends these concepts to cubic norm forms and explores applications in statistics.
